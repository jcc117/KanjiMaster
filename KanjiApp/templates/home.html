About Us

KanjiMaster.com is based on research based upon the motivations a learner has when studying a foreign language. These can range from long term to short term goals in varying degrees of seriousness. We recognize that someone who is studying Japanese to learn about pop culture will have vastly different motivations than someone who is studying it for business related purposes. Regardless, we have used research to investigate how people learn kanji and from there have implemented various means to help with kanji literacy.

It is important to note what this site covers and what it does not. KanjiMaster.com is only meant to be used as a literacy study aide. It is not meant to teach grammar, listening, speaking, or any other aspect of the Japanese language. When learning a language it is important to keep these tasks separate in order to maximize progress made in each field.

Some of the features this site includes are based around the idea of keeping the user motivated to continue learning the Japanese language. This includes using goals around the website to remind the user why they are still studying the language. A user can change these reminders at any time if these were to change. We also utilize mandatory breaks from taking quizzes. These are used not only to give the user a break and not have them burn out on studying, but also to try to encourage long-term memorization of kanji over short-term memorization. The site also breaks down the user's goals into long term and short term goals. The long term goals is updated only as the user needs to do so and is permanently displayed in the header of the user's page. Short term goals are set on a weekly basis, the user is reminded to keep these goals practical. Different cultural reference links are also included in order to keep the user interested in learning by giving them outside experience in becoming literate in a way that fits the user's personal interests.

More information about the design of the site along with more details on research can be found in the document below.

Take a Quiz

Take a quiz based on your kanji proficiency. The grades are not necessarily in order of difficulty, but rather they are just different categories upon which can choose from. To practice a certain set of kanji, select that class. Each quiz is around 30 questions. Each question is a multiple choice question asking you to match a kanji with it's romaji reading. You may take the quizzes as many times as you wish.
All kanji are take from Japanese: The Written Language Part 1 by Eleanor Harz Jorden and Mari Noda.

Outside Reading Material

Are you looking for a way to improve your reading skills in real world applications that interests you? This is the page for you!!! Here you will find links to various outside reading materials on varying difficulty levels. It is recommended that you start on some easier reading material and work you way to material that is more difficult. Remember, the journey of a thousand miles begins with a single step. Don't overwhelm yourself with material that may be too challenging for your skill level. There is no shame in admitting your limits as a second language learner. We hope you find a new way to explore your own interests in the Japanese language!
